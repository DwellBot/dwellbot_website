2) Information we collect

We collect information in three ways: (A) what you provide, (B) what we receive from third parties (like social login), and (C) what we collect automatically.

A) Information you provide

Depending on how you use the Services, you may provide:

  • Account information: name, email address, username/handle, profile image.
  • Support and communications: messages you send us, feedback, bug reports.
  • Content you submit: any text, images, or other content you choose to upload or share through the Services.
  • Payment information (if applicable): if you purchase something, payments are typically handled by a payment processor; we generally do not store full card numbers ourselves.

B) Information from social login (Google/Facebook) via Clerk

If you choose "Sign in with Google" or "Sign in with Facebook," we receive limited profile information from those providers through Clerk, which may include:

  • Email address
  • Name
  • Profile photo (if available)

We use this information to create/manage your account and authenticate you.

We do not post to your social accounts. We do not access your friends list or private messages unless you explicitly grant a permission and we clearly explain it at the time.

When you use the Services, we may automatically collect:

  • Usage data: pages/screens viewed, gameplay/session events, features used, clicks, session duration, referrers, crash logs, performance diagnostics.
  • Cookies and similar technologies: for login/session management, security, preferences, and analytics (see Section 8).

D) Real-time features (if you use them)

Some Services may include real-time features (for example: live gameplay, livestream-style viewing, chat, or other interactive sessions). If you use these features, we may process:

  • Live video and images from game cameras and/or your device camera (if enabled)
  • Audio from your device microphone (if enabled)
  • Real-time messages/events (e.g., chat messages, control inputs, session state)
  • Connection/quality telemetry (latency, packet loss, connection status) to keep sessions working well

We use this data to provide the experience, prevent abuse/fraud, and maintain safety and reliability.
